GRUE
green before t, blue after — a luminous, glass-field terminal identity
Target Brand & Style Guide · v2 · draft

GRUE is a presentation system for a modern macOS terminal built on one idea from philosophy: a color that changes what it is depending on when you look. The field is a cool, luminous glass; every accent lives on a border between two names; and one accent is bound to the clock, crossing a gradient from green through teal to blue, then falling — abruptly — into inky blue-black at the very end of dusk. Warmth is admitted, but only to draw the eye gently; it is never the ground and never the alarm.

01The concept

After Nelson Goodman's grue (the predicate "green if observed before time t, blue otherwise"), the signature color is defined by when, not just what. This is the day/night mechanism itself, so the theme needs no "dark mode" — it has a late mode, reached by living into the evening. Restraint from Tufte, proportion from Bringhurst, a deliberate argument with Rams' minimalism, and a measured techno-optimist luminance, kept on a leash and always carrying meaning.

02The temporal gradient — canonical

dawnmiddaydusknight
~/dev/atlas ⌥ main py3.12 ❯

A smooth green→teal→blue gradient across the day (no literal shimmer — just the gradient, moving), with a hard cliff at ~90% of the arc that drops the accent into inky blue-black. This form is the canonical target; in the terminal it is approximated in a few stepped stops (see §06).

Simultaneous contrast is the point: ember and the warm wash are the only warm notes, held against cool neighbours so both intensify. Grue and ember carry the two Display-P3 hues, deepened to read on a light glass ground. Chroma is scarce; it is spent on deltas.

⚑ note — the warm wash in action: a gentle, non-urgent nudge (a stale key, an advisory) reads as paper, not fire. Attention without alarm.

04Typography

  • Face: Iosevka (Term) — open-source, variable, richly ligatured; sovereign and tunable. One super-family carries hierarchy through weight and width.
  • OpenType: contextual alternates + ligatures on in prose, prompts, and the editor; off in diffs and blame so nothing is misread. Tabular figures (tnum) wherever numerals must align.
  • Weights: Light for large/quiet labels, Regular for body, Medium for the one emphasized token. Bold is never a hammer.
  • Rhythm: generous line-height (≈1.7 in the terminal), hairline rules instead of boxes, whitespace as structure.

05Information design — the laws

  • The prompt is a dashboard. Show only what changed. Clean repo → near-silent. A number, symbol, or color appears only when its state is non-default.
  • Color is taxonomy. Each hue owns one meaning (place, branch, language, add, alarm, gentle-note). A color that can't name its job is cut.
  • Ember is sacred; the warm wash is soft. Loud warmth (ember) means failure; quiet warmth (paper wash) means "notice, no rush." Never confuse them.
  • Hairlines, not borders. Hi-DPI 1px rules layer information the way a cartographer separates figure from ground.
  • Honesty over theater. The one flourish — the grue gradient — must encode something real (the time of day).

07Mechanism

  • grue-now.sh — maps the current time to a hex on the green→blue→inky gradient; a Starship custom module calls it, so the prompt accent is gradient-accurate every render.
  • grue-phase (launchd, ~every 20 min) — picks the nearest stop and rewrites the Ghostty / Zellij / bat theme files, then reloads: the stepped approximation of the arc, including the late inky stop.
  • Reversible & honest: GRUE_STATIC=1 pins a single daytime palette and disables all time behavior. Every theme is a plain dotfile; nothing is a one-way door.
